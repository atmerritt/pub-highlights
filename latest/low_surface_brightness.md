# Pub highlights
### Compact groups of galaxies in the TNG100 simulation ([Celiz et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.13293v1))
The authors study the formation and evolution of compact groups of galaxies using the TNG100 cosmological simulation. They identify these groups as FoF galaxy groups with high mean surface brightness and a minimum of 4 galaxy members. The compact groups have a median characteristic size of ∼150 kpc, 1D velocity dispersions of 150 km s^-1, and stellar masses around 2× 10^11 M_⊙. The authors find that these systems do not constitute a separate category within the broader population of galaxy groups; instead, they represent the lower end of the size distribution in the sequence of galaxy group sizes.

### The ALPINE-CRISTAL-JWST survey: spatially resolved star formation relations at $z\\sim5$ ([Accard et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.13136v1))
The authors investigate whether the resolved [CII]-SFR and KS relations established at low redshift remain valid at 4 < z < 6 by analyzing 13 main-sequence galaxies from the ALPINE and CRISTAL surveys using multi-wavelength data (HST, JWST, ALMA) at ∼2 kpc resolution. They find a resolved [CII]-SFR relation with a slope of 0.87 ± 0.15 and intrinsic scatter of 0.19 ± 0.03 dex, which is shallower and tighter than previous studies at z∼5.

### Using Doppler Imaging to model stellar activity and search for planets around Sun-like stars ([Klein et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.12963v1))
The authors aim to show that traditional DI algorithms, originally designed for rapidly-rotating stars, can also be used to model the activity of Sun-like stars when observed with new-generation highly-stable spectrographs and search for low-mass planets around them. They use DI to retrieve the relative brightness distribution at the surface of the Sun from radial velocity (RV) observations collected by HARPS-N between 2022 and 2024.

# Summary
The field of low surface brightness research has seen significant advancements in recent years, with new studies shedding light on various aspects of galaxy evolution, star formation, and planetary detection. The compact groups of galaxies identified in the TNG100 simulation represent a lower end of the size distribution in the sequence of galaxy group sizes, and their evolution is characterized by oscillatory behavior over several Gyrs during which mergers may occur.

The ALPINE-CRISTAL-JWST survey has provided new insights into spatially resolved star formation relations at z∼5, finding a resolved [CII]-SFR relation with a slope of 0.87 ± 0.15 and intrinsic scatter of 0.19 ± 0.03 dex. However, the study highlights the need for future inputs from both simulations and observations to better understand how the [CII]-to-gas conversion factor depends on local ISM properties.

In contrast, the use of Doppler Imaging (DI) to model stellar activity and search for planets around Sun-like stars has shown promising results, with DI yielding planetary mass estimates with an accuracy comparable to multi-dimensional Gaussian process regression. However, limitations of traditional DI algorithms need to be addressed to make DI a reliable alternative to state-of-the-art RV-based planet search techniques.

One key common theme across the papers is the importance of understanding the complex relationships between galaxy evolution, star formation, and planetary detection. The studies highlight the need for further research in these areas, particularly at high redshifts where observational limitations and uncertainties in locally calibrated gas tracers remain significant challenges.
