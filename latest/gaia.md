# Pub highlights
### Beyond the Nyquist frequency: Asteroseismic catalog of undersampled Kepler late subgiants and early red giants ([Liagre et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15654v1))
The authors developed a new method to identify super-Nyquist oscillators in Kepler targets and seismically characterize 285 super-Nyquist and 168 close-to-Nyquist stars with masses from 0.8 to 1.6 M_⊙. They also derive stellar ages for the sample using APOGEE spectroscopy, Gaia spectro-photometry, and stellar models.

### Thackeray's globules in IC 2944: the rocket effect revisited by ALMA ([Fernández-López et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15115v1))
The authors present an analysis of Band 3 ALMA data that reveals the cold emission associated with the neutral gas and its kinematic behavior in Thackeray's globules. They identify 46 globules, measure their kinematics, and estimate their densities and masses.

### Tidal Tails of Nearby Open Clusters. II. A Review of Simulated Properties and the Reliability of Observational Catalogues ([Jadhav et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15056v1))
The authors summarize the expected properties of tidal tails based on N-body simulations, review the reliability of tidal tail detections in the literature, and grade them according to a set of diagnostic tests.

# Summary
The field of Gaia has seen significant advancements in recent studies. One study shows that seismically characterizing stars with Kepler data is possible using new methods ([Liagre et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15654v1)). Another study reveals the cold emission associated with neutral gas and its kinematic behavior in Thackeray's globules ([Fernández-López et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15115v1)). A third study assesses the reliability of tidal tail detections using N-body simulations and observational catalogues ([Jadhav et al.](http://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15056v1)).

The key common themes across these papers include the use of new methods to analyze Gaia data, the importance of understanding the properties of tidal tails, and the need for further validation and analysis of observational catalogues.
